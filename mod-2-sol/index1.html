Akira Yoshino

Akira Yoshino (30 January 1948) is a Japanese chemist. He is a fellow of Asahi Kasei Corporation and a professor at Meijo University in Nagoya. He created the first safe, production-viable lithium-ion battery which became used widely in cellular phones and notebook computers. He was a brilliant partner to both the other. Yoshino was awarded the Nobel Prize in Chemistry in 2019 alongside M. Stanley Whittingham and John B. Goodenough.

Stanley Whittingham (22 December 1941) is a British-American chemist. He's currently a professor of chemistry, director of both the Institute for Materials Research and the Materials Science and Engineering program at Binghamton University, State University of New York.
